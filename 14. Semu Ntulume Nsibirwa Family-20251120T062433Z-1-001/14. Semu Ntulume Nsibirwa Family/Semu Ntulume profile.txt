Born to Katikirro Martin Luther Nsibirwa in 1930, Semu Kasirivu Frank Ntulume Nsibirwa had a
remarkable career as a renowned gynecologist and obstetrician known throughout Uganda later
Nairobi, Kenya.
Semu attended King’s College Budo for his primary and secondary education.
His tertiary education was then completed at Makerere University where he studied medicine.
He graduated with a double degree - Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery. His first job
was as a medical intern for the Ugandan Government in 1956 and shortly after he met Edith
Ruth Nyongereza Buluba who he married in 1957. Edith was a nurse at Mengo hospital at the
time and later the Old Mulago Hospital. The two of them would then move to the United
Kingdom where Semu pursued a post graduate degree becoming a member of the Royal
college of Obstetrician and Gynecologist. On returning to Uganda Semu became a consulting
Gynecologist with the Ugandan Government making his way up to Senior consultant, working at
various government hospitals and becoming an honorary lecturer at Makerere University
Medical school.
In 1977, Semu moved to Kenya with his family and started his medical practice in the capital
Nairobi where he also consulted at various hospitals and lectured midwives.
Patients would travel from afar seeking treatment from him, cementing his legacy as one of
Uganda’s best and brightest.
