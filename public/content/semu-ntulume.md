## Early Life and Education

Born in 1930 to Katikkiro Martin Luther Nsibirwa, Semu Kasirivu Frank Ntulume Nsibirwa grew up in a household defined by service and faith. He attended King's College Budo for both primary and secondary studies before enrolling at Makerere University Medical School, where he earned a double degreeâ€”Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery.

## Medical Career and Family

Semu began his professional journey as a medical intern with the Ugandan Government in 1956. During this time, he met nurse Edith Ruth Nyongereza Buluba at Mengo Hospital; the two married in 1957, later serving together at Old Mulago Hospital. Postgraduate training took Semu to the United Kingdom, where he became a Member of the Royal College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists. Returning to Uganda, he rose through the ranks to Senior Consultant Gynecologist, worked across multiple government hospitals, and lectured at Makerere University Medical School.

## Regional Impact

In 1977, Semu relocated with his family to Nairobi, Kenya. There he established a thriving private practice, consulted in several hospitals, and trained midwives. Patients traveled long distances for his care, cementing his reputation as one of East Africa's most trusted gynecologists and obstetricians.
